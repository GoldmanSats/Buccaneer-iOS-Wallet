Now I’m going to ask you to make a very subtle, but incredibly important aesthetic change that will give our app a premium feel. I’ve included a screenshot to give you a sense for what I want.  THE KEY DESIGN CONCEPT: "Color Diffusion"
The most important visual effect in this app is what we call color diffusion — the accent color (teal for receive, orange for send) doesn't just sit on the icon. It softly washes across the entire surface of the element, like a watercolor stain. This is what makes the app feel premium rather than flat.
How It Works on the Receive/Send Buttons
The buttons are NOT just a neutral card with a colored icon circle on top. There are three layers working together:
1. The card background itself is tinted — the entire card surface has a very pale version of the accent color baked in:
    * Receive card: #EAF7FA (pale icy teal) in light mode, or card color in dark mode
    * Send card: #FDE9E6 (pale blush pink) in light mode, or card color in dark mode
2. A full-bleed semi-transparent overlay sits on top of the card (using position: absolute; inset: 0):
    * Receive: rgba(secondary, 0.10) — a 10% wash of the secondary blue
    * Send: rgba(accent, 0.05) — a 5% wash of the coral accent
    * On hover, these increase to 20% and 10% respectively
3. The icon circle uses the accent at 20% opacity as its background:
    * Receive circle: rgba(23, 162, 184, 0.2) with #0D6E7D icon
    * Send circle: rgba(232, 106, 51, 0.2) with #B54215 icon
The combined effect: your eye sees the teal/orange concentrated at the icon, but the same hue bleeding softly all the way to the edges of the card. It's subtle but critical.
In dark mode: The card backgrounds become the standard dark card color (hsl(220, 45%, 15%)), but the overlay and icon circle still use the semi-transparent accent colors, so you still get that soft glow effect on the dark surface.
How It Works on Transaction Log Rows
Each transaction row creates color cohesion by using the SAME color family for both the icon circle (left) and the amount text (right):
* Receive rows: Icon circle = #17A2B8 at 20% opacity bg, #0D6E7D icon. Amount text = #0D6E7D. Both teal family.
* Send rows: Icon circle = #E86A33 at 20% opacity bg, #B54215 icon. Amount text = #B54215. Both orange family.
Your eye connects the colored icon on the left to the colored amount on the right, and the row "feels" like it has a color identity — even though the row background itself is transparent. The color emanates from two anchor points (icon + amount) and your brain fills in the connection between them.
What the wrong version looks like: If the icon circles are too opaque (solid colored circles instead of 20% tinted), or if the amount text is just white/default instead of the accent color, the effect breaks. You get isolated colored dots instead of a cohesive color atmosphere.
Dark Mode Specifics for Color Diffusion
In dark mode, the opacity values are HALVED to avoid the colors being too bright on the dark background:
* Icon circles: 10% opacity instead of 20% (e.g., rgba(23, 162, 184, 0.1) instead of 0.2)
* Hover states: 20% instead of 30%
* This keeps the diffusion subtle and moody rather than garish
